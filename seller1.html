~About Dilmah~


Ceylon tea is distributed all over the world under the Sri Lankan brand name Dilmah. Merrill J. Fernando started the firm in 1988. The initial names of Fernando's sons Dilhan and Malik were combined to create the name Dilmah. There are more than 100 nations where it is sold, including Estonia, the United Kingdom, Turkey, Lithuania, Malaysia, Singapore, Pakistan, Poland, Russia, Hungary, Canada, Chile, Argentina, Portugal, South Africa, Australia, India, Indonesia, Japan, the United States, Saudi Arabia, Switzerland, Taiwan, and New Zealand. The sixth-largest tea brand in the world in 2009 was thought to be Dilmah.

Fernando purchased his first few tea plantations in 1974 to try his hand at making Sri Lankan tea, and he established Ceylon Tea Services Limited in 1981.Merril Fernando persuaded Coles, an Australian grocery chain, to carry his Dilmah brand of tea in 1985. He convinced a Coles buyer to stock his tea after talking to him about it. Dilmah first appeared on the shelves of a Coles supermarket in Melbourne in 1988. Later, it moved to 35 additional Coles stores in Victoria, after which Woolworths began carrying the item. Later, Dilmah introduced the tea to Europe, North America, and New Zealand, where it was the most popular brand in 2013. Australia accounted for 10% of Dilmah's annual worldwide retail sales in 2013.The parent firm of Dilmah, Ceylon Tea Services, reported earnings of US$5.2 million on US$19.7 million in September 2009, an increase from US$1.3 million on US$18.6 million in revenues the previous year.In order to "integrate environmental and animal conservation efforts into the work of the MJF Charitable Foundation, which largely focuses on social justice," Dilmah Conservation was established in 2007.
