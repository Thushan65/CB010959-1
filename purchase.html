Give A Kind Donation


One of the middle-income countries most susceptible to the COVID-19 pandemic's economic effects is Sri Lanka. The amount of money a nation makes from exports is a major way that vulnerabilities might grow. Although Sri Lanka is not particularly vulnerable to interruptions in the global supply chain, it is problematic that it depends on a small number of exports and export destinations. One of the areas affected is Sri Lanka's export agriculture sector, which is dominated by tea, rubber, and coconut. They generate 20% of all export revenue each year, with tea exports alone accounting for around 12%. Sri Lanka's tea export revenue and production had already been on the decline for a few years by the time COVID-19 struck. Production levels have been impacted over time by unfavourable weather patterns as well as structural problems that have persisted for a while, like labour shortages and a lack of technological adoption.
